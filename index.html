Edward Yang

Pioneer of Taiwanese New Wave

Here's a time line of Edward Yang's life:

  • 1947 - Born in Shanghai, China
  • 1949 - Moves to Taipei, Taiwan with his family
  • 1974 - Receives his Masters Degree in Electrical Engineering at the University of Florida
  • 1981 - Yang eventually returned to Taiwan to write the script for and serve as a production aide on a Hong Kong TV Movie, The Winter of 1905
  • 1982 - Yang's break came when he was asked to direct and write a short, "Desires" (also known as "Expectation"), in the seminal Taiwanese New Wave collection In Our Time
  • 1983 - Yang's first feature film, That Day, on the Beach, was a fractured modernist narrative reflecting on couples and families that spliced time-lines
  • 1985 - Yang followed with his second feature film, Taipei Story, where he cast fellow auteur Hou Hsiao-hsien as the lead, a former Little-League baseball star named Lung trying to find his way in Taipei
  • 1986 - Yang's third feature film was Terrorizers, a complex multi-narrative urban thriller that reflected on city life and contained the crime elements and alienation themes of an Antonioni film
  • 1991 - Yang's fourth film was A Brighter Summer Day, a sprawling examination of youth-teen gangs, 1949 Taiwanese societal developments, and American pop-culture starring a then 15-year old Chang Chen
  • 1994 - Yang's fifth film was A Confucian Confusion, a multi-character comedy set in urban Taiwan, which was nominated for a Palme d'Or and in competition at the 1994 Cannes Film Festival and also garnered a Golden Horse Award for Best Screenplay Originally Written for The Screen
  • 1996 - Yang's sixth film was Mahjong, a sharp, incisive reflection of modern urban-Taiwan seen through foreign eyes, which also starred several foreign actors, which won an Honourable Mention at the 46th Berlin International Film Festival, where it was also nominated for the Golden Berlin Bear award
